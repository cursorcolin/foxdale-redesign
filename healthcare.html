Quality Healthcare You Can Trust

The right support, at the right time.

The Medical Home, therapy services, personal care, skilled nursing, and memory support are all available on campus. Residents can receive additional support without leaving familiar routines and relationships behind.

Care is coordinated around each resident’s needs, with a focus on comfort, independence, and continued connection to community life.

A Continuum of Care

Care that feels like home, right on campus.

Both licensed care neighborhoods sit adjacent to the Community Center, so residents stay connected to the people, activities, and routines they enjoy.

Darlington House

A licensed personal care neighborhood with private rooms, offering assistance with daily living in a warm, supportive setting — with the Community Center right at your doorstep.

Anthony House

A licensed skilled nursing neighborhood with private rooms and baths, 24-hour professional nursing, and regular physician care.

Memory Support — Two Levels

Two dedicated memory support neighborhoods: one for early stages of memory loss, and a second offering enhanced memory support — an interactive, engaging environment at every stage.

Rehabilitation & Respite

Short-term rehabilitation and respite stays provide individualized support for recovery and comfort. Both are open to Foxdale residents and the greater State College community.

Darlington House, Anthony House, and short-term rehab welcome non-residents directly. For current availability, call (814) 272-2222 or email hcadmissions@foxdalevillage.org.

Advice from Our Experts

What’s your next move?

How do you know it’s time — and what should you look for? Our Director of Health Services offers a few honest tips for families weighing their options.

Understand your options

Not all retirement communities offer the same services. A Life Plan Community provides access to additional care on the same campus if a resident’s needs change.

Plan before care becomes urgent

Moving before care needs progress can help residents remain independent longer and give families more time to compare options thoughtfully.

Know the signs

An unkempt house, missed medications, increased falls — and above all, growing isolation. Often a little daily help restores the energy for what matters most.

Questions about timing or care levels? Call the Residency Planning Office at (814) 272-2146 or toll-free (800) 253-4951.

Questions about care and coverage?

Our Residency Planning Counselors can explain care levels, Lifecare, and questions to ask your insurer. Long-term care coverage varies by policy.
