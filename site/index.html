Welcome to Foxdale Village

A community built on friendship, dignity, and respect.

Founded in 1985 by members of the State College Friends Meeting, Foxdale Village is a nonprofit Life Plan Community guided by Quaker values. Community is at the heart of life here — residents enjoy a vibrant, engaging lifestyle within neighborhoods offering independent living, personal care, skilled nursing, and memory support.

We invite you to visit, meet our residents and staff, and experience the warmth and hospitality that so many have come to call home.

The Community Center is the hub of daily life: the Café and Dining Room, library, auditorium, art studio, woodshop, billiards, gift shop, and even a model railroad room.

Cottages & Apartments

Come home to comfortable living.

Choose from two distinctive home styles — classic cottage homes in nine garden neighborhoods, or apartment homes in our welcoming three-story building. Every residence is connected to the Community Center by covered or enclosed walkways, so you stay part of community life year round.

Eleven thoughtfully designed floor plans range from cozy studios to spacious two-bedroom, two-bath homes with sunrooms.

A Continuum of Care

Giving you peace of mind.

Dependable, high-quality healthcare is one of the most important considerations when choosing a Life Plan Community. At Foxdale, an on-site Medical Home, therapy services, and our licensed care neighborhoods — Darlington House and Anthony House — are right on campus, steps from the Community Center.

Whatever the future brings, the right support is here, within a community you know and trust.

Our Foundation

Guided by Quaker values.

Mission

Foxdale Village is a nonprofit Life Plan Community guided by Quaker values that takes a community-based, holistic approach to providing high quality services at a fair cost for the well-being of older people.

Vision

Foxdale Village is recognized as a leader in aging services known for its vibrant and creative approaches to enhancing quality of life in our community.

Values

Our organization’s values — community, acceptance, engagement, inclusion, caring, fulfillment, and stewardship — reflect our founding heritage.
