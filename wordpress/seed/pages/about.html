Guided by Quaker values since 1985.

Founded by members of the State College Friends Meeting, Foxdale Village has embraced friendship, dignity, and respect for every individual for more than 40 years.

Our Story

A nonprofit community, built on Quaker heritage.

Foxdale Village began in 1985 as the dream of members of the State College Friends Meeting, modeled after communities founded in the Quaker tradition in southeastern Pennsylvania. Construction began in 1987 and was completed in 1990. Today the community includes 148 cottages and 57 apartments, all connected to the Community Center.

Our organization’s values — community, acceptance, engagement, inclusion, caring, fulfillment, and stewardship — reflect that founding heritage.

Our mission is to support the well-being of older people through high-quality services, fair costs, and a community-based, holistic approach.

Residents don’t just live here — they help govern here, initiating their own activities, serving on Residents Association committees, and sitting on the Board of Trustees.
